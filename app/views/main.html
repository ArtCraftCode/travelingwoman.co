Traveling Woman

A uniquely simple lace shawl.

Pattern Instructions

Traveling Woman is a popular, easy-to-work knitting pattern that is well suited for advanced and beginning knitters. It has more than 8,000 projects on Ravelry!

Originally released as a free pattern, Traveling Woman is easy to customize and knit in any size you like. The free pattern includes a formula for customizing the shawl size, but it's not always as easy as plugging numbers into a calculator. In an effort to make the pattern more accessible, I've created this pattern generator.

Traveling Woman is also available in a premium version. The premium version ($6 USD) comes with written and charted instructions for 11 sizes in 5 different yarn weights. This pattern generator is not the same as the premium version of the pattern and does not serve as a replacement for the content in the premium pattern. The premium version also contains additional options for customization, such as eyelets and unique charts for lighter weight yarns.

Pattern

The generator does not calculate yardage. If you're looking for yardage estimates, see the premium version of the pattern ($6).

Always slip markers as you come to them. I have indicated where to slip the marker in the set-up rows, but beyond that, you will need to remember. All stitches should be slipped knitwise unless indicated.

The shawl gets its unique shallow shape from the increases. In the stockinette stitch portion on right side rows, you increase four stitches. On wrong side rows, you increase two stitches. In the lace portion, you increase two stitches on both right and wrong side rows (you omit the increases along the center stitch).

Materials

Yarn
Whatever you want! I suggest you use a needle several sizes larger than called for by the band. For fingering weight yarns, I recommend a US 6 (4.0mm) needle.

Gauge
Up to you, but the looser your gauge the more drape.

Notions
4 stitch markers and a darning needle.
